Court Verdict Classification and Prediction in Thai Juvenile and Family Courts

Project Name

Court Verdict Classification and Prediction in Thai Juvenile and Family Courts(การพัฒนาโมเดลทำนายและจำแนกโทษของศาลเยาวชน และครอบครัว)

Project Details

The project involves developing a classification and prediction system model for offenders. Developed four deep learning models (Dense, CNN, BERT, and WangChanBERTa) to be used by the juvenile court specifically using real data from 75 features, with 3 types of data: numerical data, categorical data, and textual data to predict the punishment that youth offenders might receive. This is divided up into 9 penalty groups using: 1. Data preprocessing techniques and statistical methods on numerical data and categorical data; and 2. Text preprocessing technique on textual data, in order to develop a model that can accurately predict the outcome of a case. From the experimental results, it was found that the model Dense used to train on data that contains all 3 types of features has given the best results. Compared to other models in past research, with an accuracy of 79% and an F-1 score of 78%.

Role

Data Scientist

Responsibilities

In my role, I carefully worked with data from juvenile and family courts. Initially, I examined the data closely to identify the necessary actions for each type. This detailed examination helped me prepare the data, which involved cleaning, modifying as necessary, and selecting the most crucial elements for sound decision-making. I improved text data by correcting any spelling errors, eliminating unnecessary punctuation, removing stopwords, and discarding words that didn't add significant meaning. This process made the information clearer and more applicable. I utilized specific methods like Bag of Words, Word2Vec, and TF-IDF to dissect and comprehend complex linguistic data. This was instrumental in creating and enhancing models capable of predicting outcomes. After that created model using Bi-GRU, CNN, BERT, and WangChanBERTa. These models were meticulously developed and evaluated to ensure their accuracy and reliability for use in court decisions.
